Interests

I am interested in developing robust and efficient computational methods for real world prblems with solid mathematical foundation. My graduate work mainly focuses on devleoping novel analytical tools for hybridizable discontinuous Galerkin methods with applications to elastic/viscoelastic problems and Maxwell equations. Before that, I have worked on multiscale modeling and inverse problems.

Current interests

Discontinuous Galerkin (DG) methods

  • Hybridizable DG (HDG) methods
  • Projection-based analysis

Elastic/viscoelastic and electromagnetic waves

  • Time-domain and Laplace domain estimates
  • Model coupling and fractional time derivatives

Other experiences

Multiscale modeling

  • Molecular dynamics simulation
  • Calculation of Cauchy stress based on Irving-Kirkwood formulations

Inverse problems

  • Regularization by projections
